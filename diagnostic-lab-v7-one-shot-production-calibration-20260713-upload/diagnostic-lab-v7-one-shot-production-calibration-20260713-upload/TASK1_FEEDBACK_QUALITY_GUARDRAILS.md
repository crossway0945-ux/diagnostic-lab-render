# Task 1 Feedback Quality Guardrails

Internal production note for IELTS Writing 7+ Diagnostic Lab.

## Core Principle

Task 1 targeted revisions must be clear, specific, accurate, concise, complete, visually grounded, structurally controlled, and academically appropriate.

A revision is not acceptable if it is vaguer than the student's original sentence, nearly copies the prompt with one reporting verb changed, changes the visual meaning, invents unsupported causes or purposes, or teaches a generic reusable template.

## Introduction Revisions

A strong Task 1 introduction revision should preserve the essential available details:

- visual type or visual types
- main subject
- measurement or information type
- categories or groups
- time period
- location
- unit
- population or sample group
- comparison dimension
- process, product, structure, or function where applicable

Do not mechanically force every field into one overloaded sentence. The goal is concise completeness.

## Visual-Type Rules

- Line graphs: name what changes over time, the groups/categories, period, and unit where useful.
- Bar charts: distinguish numbers, amounts, proportions, categories, groups, places, and time points.
- Pie charts: use proportion, share, percentage, or distribution accurately.
- Tables: state what figures are compared and across which rows, columns, groups, or periods.
- Maps/plans: describe visible layout changes, years/stages, replacements, additions, removals, and unchanged features. Do not infer reasons or benefits.
- Processes/cycles: use stages, sequence, start point, endpoint/final product, and cycle language where appropriate. Do not use trend/highest/lowest language unless there is real numerical data.
- Structural/mechanism diagrams: distinguish components, structure, function, and mechanism.
- Mixed/combination graphs: name each visual separately when they perform different functions.

## Mixed-Graph Specificity

For mixed visuals, avoid broad openings such as "the visuals show" unless the sentence immediately specifies each visual's function.

Preferred pattern:

`The pie chart illustrates..., while the bar chart compares...`

For the Eva dance-class regression, the safe model is:

`The pie chart illustrates the proportion of dance classes held in four different locations in an Australian town, while the bar chart compares the numbers of young people attending ballet, tap and modern dance classes across two age groups: under 11 and 11-16.`

## Prompt-Overlap Rule

Do not accept an introduction revision that keeps nearly the same clause order and content words as the prompt while changing only one reporting verb.

Technical terms may stay when needed, including solar panel, carbon dioxide, private studios, ballet, modern dance, age group, water pipe, percentage, and metric tons.

Accuracy and clarity outrank lexical variation.

## Overview Revisions

Overview revisions must identify the dominant visual story without unsupported cause, purpose, benefit, evaluation, or prediction.

Use visual-type-appropriate language:

- charts/tables: dominant category, highest/lowest, major increase/decrease, main contrast, exception
- maps: dominant transformation, removal/replacement/addition, unchanged area, shift in land use
- processes: number of stages where clear, start, endpoint/final product, linear/cyclical nature
- mixed visuals: include a safe feature from each central visual

## Body Revisions

A body targeted revision must repair the full reporting logic when necessary:

- correct visual
- correct category or group
- correct year/time
- correct unit or figure
- correct direction, ranking, or comparison
- correct mechanism for diagrams/processes
- objective Task 1 tone
- complete grammar and tense control

If the original sentence has one local lexical issue, a minimal correction is enough. If the route or data logic is broken, the model revision should reconstruct the sentence safely.

## Paragraph Count

Do not tell students that Task 1 must always have exactly four paragraphs.

Four paragraphs are usually economical: introduction, overview, body 1, body 2. Five paragraphs are not automatically wrong if each paragraph has a clear reporting function. A short extra structure paragraph can be described as less compact, not as an automatic IELTS rule violation.

## Regression Cases

- Eva mixed graph: reject vague "the visuals show" introduction models; require separate pie-chart and bar-chart functions.
- Solar panel diagram: reject near-copy "The diagrams illustrate the structure of a solar panel and show how it can be used to heat air and water." Prefer a structure-changing model that retains the technical noun.
- Line graph: do not reduce a revision to "The graph shows changes over time." Preserve metric, groups, and period.
- Maps: do not infer purpose or benefit. Preserve existing strict caps for genuinely wrong dominant transformations.
- Processes: do not describe process diagrams as having main trends.
- Task 2 must remain unchanged.
